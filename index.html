3 · Read the story

The message that travelled.

Read like you are inside the chat. Then check who said what—and why it mattered.

Reading focus: Notice the difference between the original intention, the action, and the result.

Family chat · Tuesday

Deniz 09:14

Ece, can I tell you something? I might move to another school next term. Please keep it between us. I want to tell Arda myself.

Ece 09:16

Of course. I won’t tell anyone. We’ll figure it out together.

Maya 09:28

Ece, why do you look worried? Is everything okay?

Ece 09:29

Deniz might move schools—but please don’t say anything. I only told you because I needed advice.

Maya 09:35

I’m sorry. Arda already knows. I let it slip when he asked why Deniz was upset.

Deniz 09:40

Arda just called me. I wish I had told him myself. Ece, I trusted you. Now I feel embarrassed and disappointed.

Ece 09:42

You’re right. I regret telling Maya. I should have kept my word. Can we talk after school?

P.S. A secret can travel faster than we expect.

4 · Wish or regret?

Imagine a different reality.

Choose the time first. Are you unhappy about now, looking back at the past, or naming the action you regret?

Turkish shortcut: “Keşke…” usually leads to I wish…; “...dığıma pişmanım” often leads to I regret + -ing.
Present wish
I wish + past simple

I wish you trusted me.

= I want you to trust me now.

Keşke bana güvenseydin / güveniyor olsaydın.

Past regret
I wish + past perfect

I wish I had told Arda.

= I didn’t tell him, and I regret it.

Keşke Arda’ya söylemiş olsaydım.

General regret
regret + -ing / noun

I regret telling Maya.

I regret my decision.

Maya’ya söylediğime pişmanım.

7 · Friends or family?

Advice changes the story.

Choose the strongest response. Then defend your choice with a reason and a possible result.

1

Your sibling knows your friend is making a risky decision. Who should speak first?

2

A friend tells you a secret that suggests they are unsafe. Should the secret stay private?

3

Your cousin shared your news without asking. What should happen before you forgive them?

4

You gave poor advice and now regret it. What would you say?

“Family gives better advice.”

  • They know your history.
  • They may protect you more.
  • They often have more life experience.

“Friends understand you better.”

  • They share your daily life.
  • They may understand your generation.
  • It can feel easier to speak openly.

Matching

keep it between us → keep information private; let it slip → reveal accidentally; keep your word → do what you promised; break someone’s trust → make them stop trusting you; own up → admit a mistake; make it right → repair harm.

Teaching note

Accept was and formal were after I wish. For the safety dilemma, the intended answer is to involve a trusted adult if someone may be unsafe. Do not invite learners to disclose genuine secrets.
